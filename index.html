Your Metabolism Isn't What It Used To Be — And That's Normal

After age 35-40, most adults experience a natural decline in basal metabolic rate of 2-3% per decade. Insulin sensitivity decreases, cortisol levels from chronic stress promote fat storage (particularly visceral fat), and leptin resistance develops, blunting the 'I'm full' signal. This isn't a character flaw — it's documented physiology.

The result? Weight gain despite eating the same foods, stubborn fat that won't budge, constant cravings even when you've eaten enough, and energy crashes that make it hard to stay active. You know the feeling of looking in the mirror and wondering why your body isn't responding like it used to.

That's why VitaGLP takes a different approach. Instead of masking symptoms or promising unrealistic results, it addresses the underlying mechanisms: supporting glucose metabolism with Berberine, enhancing fat oxidation with EGCG, and helping regulate appetite signals with Chromium. No hype. Just ingredients that have been studied for their role in metabolic support.

Each ingredient was chosen based on existing research into metabolic function — not marketing trends.

A plant alkaloid studied for its potential role in supporting insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism — which means more stable energy levels throughout the day and fewer blood sugar spikes that trigger cravings.

Green Tea Extract (EGCG)

Contains epigallocatechin gallate, a catechin studied for its potential to activate AMPK enzyme and support fat oxidation — which means your body may be better able to use stored fat as an energy source.

Chromium Picolinate

An essential trace mineral involved in macronutrient metabolism and blood sugar regulation — which means better control over sugar cravings and more consistent energy without the afternoon crashes.

Apple Cider Vinegar

Vitamin B12

Essential for energy production at the cellular level and supports normal metabolic function — which means better conversion of food into usable energy, helping combat fatigue and support daily activity levels.

Ceylon Cinnamon

Contains polyphenols studied for their potential role in supporting healthy blood sugar levels and metabolic function — which means additional support for glucose metabolism alongside the other ingredients in the formula.
